# Stage 02 - Develop Audit Plan

With the scope, objectives, and criteria fixed in Stage 01, this stage turns them into a concrete
plan for carrying out the audit. It answers the practical questions: when does the audit happen,
who is involved, what gets examined, and how.

This stage puts the organisation's standing Internal Audit Programme (Clause 9.2.2) into action for
one specific audit cycle. The programme sets the general rules; the documents here apply them to
this audit.

## Contents

| Folder | File | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `01_Audit_Plan` | `Audit_Plan.docx` | The plan for this audit: scope, objectives, criteria, team and roles, timeline, and the areas to be audited. |
| `02_Audit_Schedule` | `Audit_Schedule.xlsx` | Session-by-session timetable for the fieldwork week, with dates, times, areas, auditees, and methods. |
| `03_Auditee_List` | `Auditee_List.xlsx` | The people to be interviewed, their ISMS responsibilities, and the areas each one covers. |
| `04_Audit_Methodology` | `Audit_Methodology.docx` | How the audit is conducted: methods, sampling, evidence handling, and how findings are classified. |

## Key facts for this audit cycle

- Audit dates: 16 - 20 March 2026 (one fieldwork week).
- Lead auditor: Nirmay Soni (Internal Auditor).
- Coverage: full ISMS scope (Clauses 4 to 10 and the applicable Annex A controls).
- Methods: document review, interviews, observation, sampling, and control testing.

## How it feeds the rest of the audit

The areas and methods set here drive the checklists built in Stage 03. The schedule and auditee
list guide the fieldwork in Stage 05. The finding categories defined in the methodology are the
ones used when results are recorded in Stage 06.
